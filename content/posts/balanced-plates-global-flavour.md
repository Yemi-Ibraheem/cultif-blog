Nutrition gets easier when the plate has contrast. Think protein, fiber, color, sauce, crunch, and something bright.

## Start With the Anchor

Choose the protein or main vegetable first. Everything else should support it with texture and flavor.

## Use Sauce as a Bridge

A good sauce can connect simple ingredients. Try yogurt with herbs, peanut and lime, tomato and pepper, or tahini with lemon.

## Let Culture Lead

Instead of forcing every meal into the same template, borrow patterns from cuisines you love: rice bowls, mezze plates, stews, wraps, noodle salads, or grilled spreads.
