Most meal plans fail because they treat food like a spreadsheet. Culture gives the week a rhythm: ingredients repeat, but the meals still feel distinct.

## Pick a Culture, Then a Pattern

Start with one cuisine or region for the week. Choose a grain, two proteins, two sauces, and vegetables that work across several dishes. This keeps shopping efficient while leaving room for variety.

## Reuse Components, Not Meals

Cook once, assemble differently. A spiced chickpea stew can become dinner with rice, lunch in a wrap, and a side with roasted vegetables.

## Keep One Fresh Element

Fresh herbs, citrus, pickles, salad, or a quick sauce can make leftovers feel deliberate instead of recycled.
